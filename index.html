Mosses matter!

Mosses are small, non-vascular plants that play an important ecological role in many environments. They help to regulate water cycles by absorbing and retaining water, preventing erosion and providing moisture to the soil. Mosses are also important indicators of environmental health,as they are sensitive to changes in temperature, moisture, and pollution levels.In addition, mosses provide important habitats for a variety of insects and other small animals, as well as providing food for some animals.

They also have historical and cultural significance, having been used for centuries in traditional medicine, as a source of fuel, and even as insulation.By studying and understanding mosses, we can gain insight into the health and functioning of ecosystems, as well as their historical and cultural significance.
